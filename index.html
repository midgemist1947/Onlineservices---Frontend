The Problem

A Crisis of Colossal Scale

While millions go hungry, a third of all food produced globally never reaches a table.

67M Tons Annual Waste

India generates 67 million tonnes of food waste each year — that's over 15% of the country's total food production, costing the economy $14+ billion annually.

Unsold Surplus

Restaurants, bakeries, and hotels discard edible food daily simply because of end-of-day surplus, cosmetic imperfections, or over-preparation.

795M Undernourished

Globally, 795 million people are undernourished. The food wasted in India alone could feed millions — if only it reached them in time.

The Missing Link

Existing solutions are either paid-only (Good to Grab) or manual volunteer-run (No Food Waste). Nobody has combined both into one automated platform — until now.

How It Works

Three Simple Steps

From restaurant shelf to someone's meal — in under 90 minutes.

01

Restaurant Lists Surplus

At day-end, restaurants log surplus food: item name, quantity, a discounted price (or ₹0 for donation), and a pickup window — typically 90 minutes.

02

Auto-Matched & Claimed

Nearest users get notified first. Regular users see discounted listings; verified NGOs see donation posts. Unclaimed food auto-escalates to NGOs after a time threshold.

03

Pickup & Confirm

Claimant picks up in person. Restaurant scans a simple OTP or QR code for proof of collection — no delivery waste, no paperwork, full accountability.

Food Safety & Liability

Trust is Our Main Ingredient

We take food safety seriously. Every listing on Anndaan follows strict guidelines.

FSSAI Guidelines

All listings comply with FSSAI surplus food handling recommendations — time & temperature limits, mandatory preparation-time fields, and safe storage guidelines.

Good Samaritan Protection

India's draft Good Samaritan law shields good-faith food donors from liability. We integrate disclaimers and legal safeguards so restaurants can donate with confidence.

FAQ

Got Questions?

Is Anndaan legally safe for restaurants?

Yes. We provide a liability waiver template and follow FSSAI guidelines. India's Good Samaritan law (under discussion) will further protect good-faith donors.

How does the OTP/QR handoff work?

When a user claims a listing, a unique 4-digit OTP and QR code are generated. At pickup, the restaurant scans/verifies the code to confirm handoff.

What happens if nobody claims a paid listing?

It auto-escalates to the NGO track after the pickup window. Nearby verified NGOs get a priority notification.

How do you verify NGOs?

NGOs must submit their registration certificate, a government-issued ID, and address proof. We verify each within 2 business days.

What about food safety?

All listings require a preparation-time field. Perishable items have a max listing window of 2-4 hours. Our system auto-removes expired listings.

How do you make money if NGO donations are free?

We charge 5-10% on paid transactions only. NGO donations are free — keeping us eligible for CSR grants. Premium analytics tier at ₹499/month.
